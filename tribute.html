Linus Torvalds

The human behind the Linux kernel & git!

Linus Torvalds' milestones timeline:

  • 1969 - In December 28th, Linus Benedict Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1988 - Torvalds enrolled at the University of Helsinki, graduating with a master's degree in computer science.
  • 1994 - Linux Version 1.0 was released on March 14th.
  • 1996 - Tux (the official linux mascot) was created by Larry Ewing after an initial suggestion made by Alan Cox and further refined by Linus Torvalds.
  • 1998 - Torvalds received an EFF Pioneer Award.
  • 1999 - He was named by the MIT Technology Review TR100 as one of the world's top 100 innovators under age 35.
    Also, Red Hat and VA Linux, both leading developers of Linux-based software, presented Torvalds with stock options in gratitude for his creation.
  • 2000 - He was 17th in the Time Magazine's "Time 100: The Most Important People of the Century" poll.
  • 2005 - Git development began in April. Version 1.0 was released on December 21st.
  • 2014 - The Eclipse Foundation reported in its annual community survey that as of May, Git became the most widely used source-code management tool.
  • 2015 - Stack Overflow has included Version control in their annual developer survey and Git was the overwhelming favorite of responding developers in these surveys.
